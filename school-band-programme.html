All Equipment Provided

How a 30-minute session runs

0–5 mins

Warm-up, rhythm focus, quick role allocation and setup.

5–15 mins

Instrument skill focus (one clear target skill).

15–25 mins

Band rehearsal — build and loop small sections, then join parts.

25–30 mins

Run-through + quick feedback + pack down.

Sessions are at a fixed day/time each week and aren’t rearranged for individual absences.

Term structure (standard 6-week block)

  1. Week 0: sign-ups, preferences, bands formed, schedule issued, payment confirmed.
  2. Week 1: routines set, instrument allocation confirmed, basics introduced.
  3. Weeks 2–4: core learning + rehearsal focus; build song sections gradually.
  4. Week 5: performance prep; full run-throughs; transitions tightened.
  5. Week 6: final session includes rehearsal + short showcase (10–20 mins).

Sign-up window

Sign-ups usually open 2–3 weeks before. Recommended cut-off is 7 days before Week 1. No new pupils are added after Week 1.

Equipment used (all-electric)

Designed for school rooms: controlled volume, clean setup, and safe cable management.

Core instruments

  • Electronic drums (quiet enough for school rooms)
  • 61-key touch-sensitive keyboard + sustain pedal
  • Electric guitar + strap
  • Electric bass + strap
  • Wired vocal mic (singing + announcements)

Extras (minimum)

  • Spare drum sticks
  • Picks + spare strings (guitar + bass)
  • Tuner
  • Music stands

Sound & setup

  • Mixer = the control centre (balanced levels)
  • Powered PA speaker (one for lessons; second may be added for showcases)
  • Cable safety: taped down if crossing a walkway (no exceptions)
  • Extension lead + power block + tidy layout

Typical school visit pattern

One room, three bands back-to-back, same routine every time (set-up, band 1, changeover, band 2, changeover, band 3, pack down).

Pricing & key policies

Simple, consistent rules so schools and parents know exactly what to expect.

Pricing

£15 per child / session

Charged termly in advance. Standard 6-week block = £90. Full term payment is required before Week 1 to confirm a place.

Group size

Bands are typically 4 pupils (minimum 3 to run).

Key policies

  • Missed sessions: not refundable; sessions aren’t rearranged for individual absences.
  • If Elliot’s Mobile Music cancels: a make-up session or credit is offered where reasonably possible.
  • School closures/events: sessions are credited or rescheduled where possible.
  • Late joiners: not accepted after Week 1.
  • Instrument changes: no changes after Week 1 (unless safeguarding/practical necessity).

Safeguarding & behaviour

Sessions follow school safeguarding expectations and agreed supervision arrangements. Pupils follow school behaviour rules; unsafe behaviour may pause or end a session until a plan is agreed.

Photos / videos

Only used with explicit consent. Consent can be withdrawn at any time.

End-of-term performance

Included in the programme cost and takes place during the final session. Performance details are confirmed with the school by Week 4.

What the school provides vs what Elliot’s Mobile Music provides

Provided by Elliot’s Mobile Music

  • Tutor delivery + session structure
  • Instruments (as agreed) + sound setup
  • Parent communications + termly payments
  • Attendance tracking

Provided by the school

  • Room/space
  • Timetable support
  • Help getting pupils to/from sessions
  • Safeguarding supervision arrangements (as per school policy)
Privacy (short)

Elliot’s Mobile Music (Elliot Wardill) collects basic details such as pupil name/year, parent contact, instrument preference, attendance, and any necessary notes provided — to run sessions and manage payments. Photo/video is consent-based and consent can be withdrawn.

Refunds / withdrawals

Places can usually be ended at the end of a paid term. Mid-term refunds are not normally offered unless agreed in exceptional circumstances. If a group is not viable by the cut-off (minimum numbers), families may be offered an alternative group, deferment, or a refund.
