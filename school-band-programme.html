How a 6-week block runs (Week 0–6)

Simple, repeatable structure. Schools get a predictable timetable; parents get clear expectations and a confident end-point.

  1. W0

    Week 0 — Sign-ups & band setup

    Preferences collected, bands formed, schedule issued, and payment confirmed.

  2. W1

    Week 1 — Routines & foundations

    Instruments allocated, core basics taught, and the band routine established.

  3. W2–4

    Weeks 2–4 — Build the song

    Skills + rehearsal focus. Sections are built and looped, then parts are joined together.

  4. W5

    Week 5 — Performance prep

    Full run-throughs, transitions tightened, confidence building and stage-ready practice.

  5. W6

    Week 6 — Final rehearsal + showcase

    Final session includes rehearsal and a short informal showcase (10–20 mins).

What happens in a 30-minute session

0–5 mins

Warm-up, rhythm focus, quick role allocation and setup.

5–15 mins

Instrument skill focus (one clear target skill).

15–25 mins

Band rehearsal — build and loop small sections, then join parts.

25–30 mins

Run-through + quick feedback + pack down.

Sessions are at a fixed day/time each week and aren’t rearranged for individual absences.

Sign-up window

Sign-ups usually open 2–3 weeks before. Recommended cut-off is 7 days before Week 1. No new pupils are added after Week 1.

What Elliot provides vs what the school provides

Clear responsibilities make the programme run smoothly and keep safeguarding consistent with school policy.

Provided by Elliot’s Mobile Music

  • Tutor delivery + session structure (beginner-friendly routines)
  • Instruments (as agreed) + sound setup (mixer & speaker)
  • Parent communications + sign-up + termly payments
  • Attendance tracking and simple progress notes

Provided by the school

  • Room/space suitable for a small group session
  • Timetable support and agreed session times
  • Help getting pupils to/from sessions (as per school routines)
  • Safeguarding supervision arrangements (as per school policy)

Safeguarding & Safety (summary)

Sessions follow school expectations, with a tidy, controlled setup designed for school rooms.

Safety in the room

  • Cables are routed safely; any cable crossing a walkway is taped down.
  • Volume is controlled through a mixer to keep levels comfortable and appropriate for school spaces.
  • All-electric instruments for clean setup and predictable sound levels.
  • Quick pack-down and tidy layouts to keep walkways clear.

Safeguarding approach

  • Sessions follow the school’s safeguarding expectations and agreed supervision arrangements.
  • Any concern is handled via the school’s DSL process and reported appropriately.
  • Pupils follow school behaviour rules; unsafe behaviour may pause/end a session until a plan is agreed.
  • Photos/videos are used only with explicit consent; consent can be withdrawn at any time.

Comfortable, consistent, predictable

Designed to be easy to timetable, easy to supervise, and easy for beginners to succeed — without overwhelming the school environment.

Equipment used (all-electric)

Key policies (summary)

  • Missed sessions: not refundable; sessions aren’t rearranged for individual absences.
  • If Elliot’s Mobile Music cancels: a make-up session or credit is offered where reasonably possible.
  • School closures/events: sessions are credited or rescheduled where possible.
  • Late joiners: not accepted after Week 1.
  • Instrument changes: no changes after Week 1 (unless safeguarding/practical necessity).

Showcase included

Included in the programme cost and takes place during the final session. Performance details are confirmed with the school by Week 4.
Privacy (short)

Elliot’s Mobile Music (Elliot Wardill) collects basic details such as pupil name/year, parent contact, instrument preference, attendance, and any necessary notes provided — to run sessions and manage payments. Photo/video is consent-based and consent can be withdrawn.

Refunds / withdrawals

Places can usually be ended at the end of a paid term. Mid-term refunds are not normally offered unless agreed in exceptional circumstances. If a group is not viable by the cut-off (minimum numbers), families may be offered an alternative group, deferment, or a refund.

FAQ

The most common questions from schools and parents.

Does my child need any experience?

No — the programme is designed for beginners.

Do pupils take instruments home?

Instruments are provided for in-school use during sessions.

What happens if a child misses a session?

Sessions aren’t rearranged for individual absences and are not refundable; the slot stays reserved.

How loud is it? Will it disturb nearby classes?

All-electric setup with volume controlled through a mixer. Levels can be adjusted to suit the room.

Who supervises pupils during sessions?

Safeguarding follows the school’s expectations and agreed supervision arrangements (as per school policy).

Can pupils swap instruments part-way through?

No instrument changes after Week 1 (unless needed for safeguarding/practical reasons).

What if the group doesn’t reach minimum numbers?

Minimum is 3 pupils. If not viable by cut-off: alternative group, deferment, or refund.
