Creative project offer

Make a Song turns a musical idea into a finished original track.

Make a Song is a six-week creative course for ages 6+ that helps children move from a first idea to a finished piece of music. Instead of only learning existing songs, students build something of their own and experience the full journey of creating, shaping and recording it.

The course starts with songwriting and idea-building, then moves into recording and production. By the end, the student has not only worked on musical skills, but also confidence, creativity, decision-making and the excitement of making something original.

How the six-week course works

Find the idea and direction

The course begins with ideas. That might mean lyrics, a theme, a mood, a hook, a rhythm or a simple musical idea the student wants to explore further.

Build the song itself

The next stage is about shaping those ideas into something more complete: verses, choruses, structure, pacing, and the choices that make the song feel like a real finished piece.

Record and refine

Once the song is ready, the focus shifts to recording. The student starts hearing their ideas come to life and learning how performance and production change the feel of the track.

Shape the finished result

The final stage is about refining and completing the track so the student leaves with a real sense of ownership, progress and pride in what they have created.

What students actually work on

Ideas and themes

Exploring what the song is about and what kind of mood, message or feeling it should carry.

Lyrics and melody

Turning early ideas into words, hooks and musical lines that feel memorable and personal.

Structure and arrangement

Making the song feel complete by deciding how sections fit together and how the musical parts support the idea.

Recording choices

Learning how performances, takes and production decisions affect the final feel of the song.
